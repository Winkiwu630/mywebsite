Flying moss is a miracle!

One of the more magnificent things that scientists have found in our wonderfully weird world is the Flying Moss of Cumulo. These balls of moss, much like Marimo moss balls of Japan, endlessly roll around finding the best spot to collect the suns rays to feed themselves. The strange thing that scientist still have not been able to explain is that these moss balls move around thousands of feet up in the air, moving from one mass of water particulates, that we call clouds, to the next.

These moss balls although closly related to the Marimo moss balls of Japan are very different. They move from cloud formation to cloud formation using the jet stream instead of water currents to push them. Obviously this means they grow much smaller then the Marimo moss balls which have been known to grow as large as 12". The largest Flying moss ball recovered by scientists was only 0.25" while most of them averaged around 0.1". You may be wondering how scientist even knew to look for these small interesting creatures. Over the last 20 years there has been several reports of planes striking a patch of greenery during take off or landing. Most pilots chalked this up to a group of oddly colored bugs but one daring scientist, Heir Ulga Von Hammerschmidt, wanted to investigate this phenomenon more closely. Through her careful observations she was able to identify the cellular structure similarly resembling the structure of the Marimo moss balls.

After this facinating discovery by Heir Ulga, scientist chartered private planes to attempt to collect some living specimens. These efforts resulted in the first ever captive Flying moss specimens collected by the Univerity of Hawaii.
